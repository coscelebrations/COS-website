Insider Tips for Your Treasury Wedding

After performing countless weddings at Treasury on the Plaza, here's what we've learned:

The Vault Entrance Is Everything

That massive vault door is the real deal - a genuine bank vault that takes about 20 seconds to open. We coordinate with the Treasury team to time your entrance perfectly to the music. Want a dramatic silhouette pull? We'll show the door operator exactly which lyric or beat to start on so your moment explodes exactly when it should.

The Room Flip Is Magic - Let Guests Experience It

During your cocktail hour in the Exchange Room, the Treasury team transforms the ballroom from ceremony to reception in under an hour. We recommend doing a private "room reveal" just the two of you before guests return - then watch their faces when those doors open. It never gets old.

Keep It Under Six Hours

This applies to any venue, but especially for a high-energy celebration: once you push past six hours, you need the right crowd to sustain the energy. The typical Treasury timeline (4 PM guests, 4:30 ceremony, 10 PM end) hits the sweet spot.

Ceremony Setup Is Strategic

Our DJ booth sits next to the vault, hidden behind drapes during the ceremony. But we don't go in blind - we position a satellite speaker in the back of the ballroom so we can see everything and manage the ceremony seamlessly. Treasury provides the officiant mic; we handle everything else.

The Acoustics Need Experience

Those stunning tall ceilings create an echo-y room. We've dialed in the exact EQ settings for speeches (clear and intimate) versus dancing (full and energetic). It's one of those things you only learn from doing it many times.

Live Sax + The Vault = Unforgettable

Our DJ + live saxophone combo was made for this space. During open dancing, we peek around the pillar and hit the floor with guests - the Gatsby vibe of Treasury plus live music creates something electric. It's one of our favorite rooms to perform in.

Sample Treasury Wedding Timeline

Every wedding is unique, but here's a typical flow we've refined over many weddings at Treasury on the Plaza:

1:30 PM
COS Arrives for Setup

Load in through side door by the Exchange Room. DJ booth positioned next to vault, hidden behind ceremony drapes.

4:00 PM
Guest Arrival

Guests seated in ballroom for ceremony. Soft prelude music playing.

4:30 PM
Ceremony

Ceremony in the ballroom. Treasury provides officiant mic; we handle processional, recessional, and all other audio.

5:00 PM
Cocktail Hour

Guests move to the Exchange Room. Background music: jazz, acoustic covers, or your custom playlist. Meanwhile, Treasury's team executes the incredible room flip.

5:45 PM
Couple's Room Reveal

Private moment for just the two of you to see the transformed ballroom before guests return.

6:00 PM
Doors Open + Grand Entrance

Guests return to ballroom. We introduce the wedding party, build the energy, then all eyes on the vault for your entrance. Those doors take 20 seconds to open - we time it perfectly to your song.

6:10 PM
First Dance

Right into your first dance while the energy is high.

6:15 PM
Welcome & Blessing → Dinner

Brief welcome, then dinner service begins. Background music keeps the vibe warm.

7:15 PM
Toasts

Maid of honor, best man, and any other speakers. We adjust EQ for speech clarity in the tall-ceiling room.

7:45 PM
Cake Cutting + Parent Dances

Cake cutting, then parent dances if you're having them.

8:00 PM
Open Dancing

The floor is open! If you've added live saxophone, this is where it shines - we'll be out there with your guests, peeking around the pillars and keeping the energy electric.

9:45 PM
Last Dance + Send-Off

One final song with everyone on the floor, then your grand exit.

Pro tip: This timeline gives you about 5.5 hours of reception time - the sweet spot for Treasury. Going past 10 PM is possible, but we've found this pacing keeps energy high from start to finish.
