How a Touring Musician Became North Florida's Most Sought-After Wedding DJ

Most wedding DJs got into the business through events. Corey got into it through music. He started on piano at age four, picked up saxophone shortly after, and spent years touring professionally - performing on stages across six continents and sharing lineups with some of the biggest names in music.

That background changes everything about how he approaches a wedding. Where other DJs rely on pre-built playlists and hope for the best, Corey hears a room the way a musician hears a band. He knows when the energy is building, when it needs to breathe, and exactly when to push it over the edge. It is not a formula - it is instinct built from thousands of hours performing live.

He founded COS Celebrations with his fiancee Kyle, and together they pioneered the DJ + live saxophone model in Northeast Florida. The concept was simple: what if a wedding DJ could actually play music, not just press play? That idea became the foundation of everything COS does.

Today, Corey leads a team of DJs and musicians across Jacksonville, St. Augustine, Ponte Vedra, Amelia Island, Orlando, and beyond. He still performs regularly - because after 1,000 weddings, the dance floor still gives him a rush. But he also spends time training every DJ on the COS roster, making sure the same musicianship and attention to detail shows up whether he is behind the booth or someone else on his team is.

The COS Approach

Your Wedding, Not Mine

It starts with you

Every couple is different. Every wedding should feel different. Corey builds each set around what the couple actually wants to hear - their songs, their energy, their vision for the night. There is no default playlist. There is no template. The first conversation is always about you.

Reading the room is not optional

This is the part most DJs struggle with and where Corey's touring background becomes an unfair advantage. After performing for crowds of 50 and crowds of 50,000, reading a room is second nature. He knows when to bring the energy up, when to let a moment sit, and how to keep a dance floor packed without forcing it.

The DJ should be invisible

No TV screens on the booth. No flashy ego-driven setup. No over-the-top MC voice competing with your night. Corey's philosophy is to remove all ego from the day and keep the focus where it belongs - on the couple and their guests. You should not notice the DJ. You should notice that the dance floor is packed and you are having the best night of your life.

Live music changes everything

When live saxophone weaves through a DJ set, it creates something recordings cannot replicate. The energy shifts. People stop checking their phones and start moving. This is not a gimmick - it is what happens when real musicianship meets real-time crowd connection. It is the reason COS exists.

Experiences over equipment

CO2 cannons can be fun. Cold sparks look amazing. But the foundation of a great wedding is the music and the moments. Corey does not need a bunch of toys to make a dance floor unforgettable - he needs the right song at the right time and the instinct to know the difference. The extras are extras. The music is everything.
