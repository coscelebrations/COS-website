The Lightner Museum isn't just one of St. Augustine's most photographed buildings — it's one of the most acoustically unique wedding venues in Florida. Originally the Alcazar Hotel, built in 1888 by Henry Flagler, this Spanish Renaissance Revival masterpiece features soaring ceilings, intricate Moorish details, and a grand ballroom that once housed the world's largest indoor swimming pool. As a preferred vendor at Lightner Museum, we've mastered how sound travels through the cavernous space, where to position speakers for optimal coverage during both ceremonies and receptions, and how to work seamlessly with their exceptional events team. When you book COS Celebrations for your Lightner wedding, you're getting a team that knows this historic venue inside and out.

About Lightner Museum

Venue Information

Location: 75 King Street, St. Augustine, FL 32084

Capacity: Up to 250 guests

Style: Historic museum, Spanish Renaissance Revival architecture, 1888

Website: lightnermuseum.org

The Lightner Museum occupies the former Alcazar Hotel, built by railroad magnate Henry Flagler in 1888. The building's most stunning feature is the grand ballroom — originally the hotel's indoor swimming pool, once billed as the largest in the world. Today, that pool has been converted into an elegant event space, with the original balconies now serving as seating areas overlooking the dance floor. The courtyard offers a magical setting for outdoor ceremonies, surrounded by Spanish moss-draped architecture and the historic charm that makes St. Augustine special. It's a venue that photographs beautifully and creates an atmosphere unlike any other.
