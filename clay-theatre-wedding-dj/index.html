About The Clay Theatre

Venue Information

Location: 326 Walnut Street, Green Cove Springs, FL 32043

Capacity: Up to 150 guests (ceremony, cocktail, reception)

Style: Restored Art Deco theater — white walls, black trim, crystal chandeliers, LED tray ceiling, custom marquee, Courtyard for outdoor ceremonies

Spaces: Main Space (reception with balcony entrance), Courtyard (ceremony & cocktail), Backstage Suite (getting-ready lounge)

Website: claytheatre.com

The Clay Theatre sits in downtown Green Cove Springs — a small historic town on the St. Johns River, almost exactly between Jacksonville and St. Augustine. Restored to capture Art Deco character without leaning kitschy, the venue has three distinct spaces: the Courtyard for outdoor ceremonies, the intimate Backstage Suite for getting ready, and the Main Space for the reception, complete with a balcony you can use for a grand entrance. The venue's three wedding packages — Sundance, Golden Globes, and Oscars — build up from a reception-only setup to full ceremony + cocktail + reception with upgraded lighting and that custom marquee message. It's a venue with a strong point of view, which is exactly why the entertainment has to match.

How do sparkler exits work at The Clay Theatre?

The Clay Theatre's Oscars package includes grand-exit coordination, so the venue's team and the DJ sync cold-spark cues to the final track. We time the last song build to the exact moment the sparks go up on the sidewalk out front. For Laura and Dusko's exit in May 2026, the sparks lit as the drop hit, which is the result of a pre-show timing pass with Clay's coordinator, not luck.

Can you handle both the Clay Theatre Courtyard ceremony and the Main Space reception?

Yes. Most COS bookings at Clay Theatre are full-day: ceremony sound in the Courtyard, cocktail hour music in the transition space, and the reception in the Main Space. We bring separate systems tuned for each room. The Courtyard needs directional speakers because it is open-air with street noise, while the Main Space needs a smaller, cleaner system because the room is intimate and hard-surfaced.
