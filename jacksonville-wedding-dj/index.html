What Do We Know About Jacksonville Weddings?

Ortega & the River

Timuquana's 50,000 sq ft clubhouse has been hosting weddings since 1923 - we're a preferred vendor and know exactly where to place fills in that ballroom. Epping Forest's duPont mansion sits right on the St. Johns, and that river breeze changes everything about outdoor cocktail hour sound.

Jacksonville's most interesting wedding scene right now. The Glass Factory - a 1936 Henry Klutho building - has industrial acoustics that need careful EQ. The Beaches & Ponte Vedra

Wind eats vocals. We bring the right mics and the right monitor placement. Worth knowing: most beach-adjacent venues enforce a 10 PM noise cutoff.

Jacksonville Wedding DJ FAQ

How much does a wedding DJ cost in Jacksonville?

The average Jacksonville wedding runs about $38,000 total - entertainment is typically 3-5% of that. COS packages start at $1,500 for four hours of DJ/MC. Most couples at venues like Timuquana or Epping Forest land between $1,900 and $2,400, which covers ceremony, reception, and options like live saxophone. All-in pricing, no surprise fees.

Do you charge extra for Jacksonville weddings?

No. Never. We're based in St. Augustine - Jacksonville is home turf. Jax Beach, San Marco, Riverside, Southside, Ponte Vedra - all covered, no travel fees. The quote you get is the price you pay.

What months book fastest in Jacksonville?

October through April is peak season - the opposite of northern states, since that's when Jacksonville weather is actually pleasant. Saturday nights at Timuquana and Epping Forest go fast. Pro tip: fall dates compete with Jaguars and Gators games for hotel rooms, so book early.

Which Jacksonville venues have you actually worked?

We're a preferred vendor at Timuquana Country Club, Epping Forest Yacht Club, and Casa Marina Hotel. We've also performed at The River Club, Garden Club of Jacksonville, Deerwood Country Club, The Glass Factory, and private estates throughout Ortega and San Marco.

How do I choose among 80+ Jacksonville wedding DJs?

Three things: venue experience (have they worked your space before?), reviews that mention specifics (not just "great DJ!"), and transparent pricing. Ask for an all-in quote. If they can't tell you what's included without hedging, keep looking.

Do I get control over the music?

Full control. Our planning app lets you build your playlist, flag must-plays, and list anything you want avoided. On the night, we blend your picks with live crowd reading. Your wedding, your soundtrack.

Is there a more affordable option?

Yes - our sister company AE Entertainment offers DJ-only packages starting at $800. Same team, same planning tools, same reliability - just without the live musicians. Great fit if you want quality on a tighter budget.
